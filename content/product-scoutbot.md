# ScoutBot Mobile Platform

ScoutBot is Acme Robotics' autonomous mobile robot (AMR), designed to move materials around warehouses and factory floors without fixed tracks or rails. ScoutBot navigates using a combination of LiDAR mapping and onboard cameras, allowing it to build a map of a facility on its first run and then navigate autonomously afterward.

The standard ScoutBot model can carry loads up to 150 kg on its built-in flatbed, with an optional roller-top attachment for automated conveyor hand-off. Battery life is roughly 10 hours of continuous operation, with a fast-charge feature that restores 80% capacity in 45 minutes.

ScoutBot units communicate with each other over a mesh network, so a fleet of ScoutBots can coordinate traffic and avoid collisions without a central controller. Fleet size is currently supported up to 40 units per facility.

Safety features include 360-degree obstacle detection, automatic slow-down in pedestrian zones, and an emergency stop button on all four sides of the chassis. ScoutBot is certified to the same safety standards required for collaborative robots operating near humans.

ScoutBot integrates with AcmeOS the same way the ArmLine arms do, so a facility can manage arms and mobile robots from a single dashboard.
