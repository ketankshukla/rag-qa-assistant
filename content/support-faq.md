# Support FAQ

**How do I request a repair for an ArmLine arm?**
Contact Acme Robotics support through the AcmeOS dashboard's "Support" tab, or email support@acme-robotics.example. Include the arm's serial number, which is printed on a label near the base of the unit. Most repair requests receive a response within one business day.

**What is Acme Robotics' return policy?**
Hardware purchased directly from Acme Robotics can be returned within 30 days of delivery for a full refund, provided the unit is uninstalled and in its original packaging. Custom configurations and accessory bundles are non-refundable after installation.

**Does AcmeOS work offline?**
AcmeOS requires an internet connection for initial setup and firmware updates, but once configured, the drag-and-drop programming interface and daily operation work fully offline. Fleet coordination features for ScoutBot do require a local network connection between units, though not necessarily internet access.

**How often should ArmLine arms be serviced?**
Acme Robotics recommends a preventive maintenance check every 2,000 operating hours or once per year, whichever comes first, for both the ArmLine 100 and ArmLine 500.

**Can I run third-party grippers on an ArmLine arm?**
Yes, as long as the gripper uses a standard ISO 9409-1-50-4-M6 mounting flange. Acme Robotics cannot guarantee compatibility or safety certification for non-Acme grippers, so customers use them at their own risk.
