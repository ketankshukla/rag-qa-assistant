# Company Overview

Acme Robotics was founded in 2016 with a simple mission: make industrial automation affordable for small manufacturers. The company started in a garage in Austin, Texas, with three engineers and a single robotic arm prototype.

Today, Acme Robotics builds modular robotic arms, autonomous mobile robots, and the AcmeOS software platform that ties them together. The company has grown to 140 employees across offices in Austin, Toronto, and Berlin.

Acme Robotics' core product line includes the ArmLine 100 (a lightweight collaborative arm for small parts assembly), the ArmLine 500 (a heavier-duty arm rated for continuous industrial use), and the ScoutBot mobile platform for warehouse transport.

The company's headquarters remains in Austin, where the main manufacturing floor and R&D lab are located. Customer support is handled from the Toronto office, and the Berlin office focuses on European sales and localization.

Acme Robotics' CEO is Priya Nandakumar, who co-founded the company and previously worked as a robotics researcher at a national laboratory. The CTO, Marcus Ferreira, leads the engineering team and holds several patents in robotic gripper design.
